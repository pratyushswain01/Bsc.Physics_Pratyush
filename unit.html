Topic 1.1: Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

Simple Harmonic Motion is a special type of periodic motion or oscillation where the restoring force is directly proportional to the displacement and acts in the direction opposite to that of displacement.

Key Concepts

  • Oscillatory Motion: A motion that repeats itself over a period of time.
  • Restoring Force: The force that brings the object back to its mean or equilibrium position. In SHM, F = -kx, where k is the force constant and x is the displacement.
  • Amplitude (A): The maximum displacement or distance moved by a point on a vibrating body or wave measured from its equilibrium position.
  • Time Period (T): The time taken to complete one full oscillation.
  • Frequency (f): The number of oscillations per unit time. f = 1/T.

Mathematical Formulation

From Newton's second law, F = ma. For SHM, we have:

ma = -kx

This leads to the second-order differential equation that defines SHM:

d²x/dt² + (k/m)x = 0

The general solution to this equation is:

x(t) = A cos(ωt + φ)

Where ω = sqrt(k/m) is the angular frequency and φ is the phase constant.
