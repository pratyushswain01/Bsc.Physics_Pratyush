Fourier Series & Parseval's Identity

This page summarizes the standard formulas for calculating Fourier series for functions defined over different intervals, including special cases for even and odd functions (half-range series) and the associated Parseval's identities.

General Fourier Series

1. Fourier Series in (0, 2π)

$$ f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} [a_n \cos(nx) + b_n \sin(nx)] $$ Where the coefficients are: $$ a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{2\pi} f(x) \,dx $$ $$ a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{2\pi} f(x) \cos(nx) \,dx $$ $$ b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{2\pi} f(x) \sin(nx) \,dx $$

2. Fourier Series in (-π, π)

$$ f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} [a_n \cos(nx) + b_n \sin(nx)] $$ Where the coefficients are: $$ a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \,dx $$ $$ a_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \cos(nx) \,dx $$ $$ b_n = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} f(x) \sin(nx) \,dx $$

3. Fourier Series in (0, 2L)

$$ f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left[ a_n \cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) + b_n \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \right] $$ Where the coefficients are: $$ a_0 = \frac{1}{L} \int_{0}^{2L} f(x) \,dx $$ $$ a_n = \frac{1}{L} \int_{0}^{2L} f(x) \cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \,dx $$ $$ b_n = \frac{1}{L} \int_{0}^{2L} f(x) \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \,dx $$

4. Fourier Series in (-L, L)

$$ f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left[ a_n \cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) + b_n \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \right] $$ Where the coefficients are: $$ a_0 = \frac{1}{L} \int_{-L}^{L} f(x) \,dx $$ $$ a_n = \frac{1}{L} \int_{-L}^{L} f(x) \cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \,dx $$ $$ b_n = \frac{1}{L} \int_{-L}^{L} f(x) \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \,dx $$

Series for Even and Odd Functions

5. Even Function in (-π, π) — Cosine Series

If $f(x)$ is even, $b_n = 0$ for all $n$.

$$ f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \cos(nx) $$ $$ a_0 = \frac{2}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} f(x) \,dx $$ $$ a_n = \frac{2}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} f(x) \cos(nx) \,dx $$

6. Odd Function in (-π, π) — Sine Series

If $f(x)$ is odd, $a_0 = 0$ and $a_n = 0$ for all $n$.

$$ f(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n \sin(nx) $$ $$ b_n = \frac{2}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} f(x) \sin(nx) \,dx $$

7. Even Function in (-L, L) — Cosine Series

If $f(x)$ is even, $b_n = 0$ for all $n$.

$$ f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) $$ $$ a_0 = \frac{2}{L} \int_{0}^{L} f(x) \,dx $$ $$ a_n = \frac{2}{L} \int_{0}^{L} f(x) \cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \,dx $$

8. Odd Function in (-L, L) — Sine Series

If $f(x)$ is odd, $a_0 = 0$ and $a_n = 0$ for all $n$.

$$ f(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) $$ $$ b_n = \frac{2}{L} \int_{0}^{L} f(x) \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \,dx $$

Half-Range Series

9. Half-Range Cosine Series in (0, π)

$$ f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \cos(nx) $$ $$ a_0 = \frac{2}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} f(x) \,dx \quad , \quad a_n = \frac{2}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} f(x) \cos(nx) \,dx $$

10. Half-Range Sine Series in (0, π)

$$ f(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n \sin(nx) $$ $$ b_n = \frac{2}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} f(x) \sin(nx) \,dx $$

11. Half-Range Cosine Series in (0, L)

$$ f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) $$ $$ a_0 = \frac{2}{L} \int_{0}^{L} f(x) \,dx \quad , \quad a_n = \frac{2}{L} \int_{0}^{L} f(x) \cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \,dx $$

12. Half-Range Sine Series in (0, L)

$$ f(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) $$ $$ b_n = \frac{2}{L} \int_{0}^{L} f(x) \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) \,dx $$

Parseval's Identity

13. Identity in (0, 2π)

$$ \int_{0}^{2\pi} [f(x)]^2 \,dx = \pi \left[ \frac{a_0^2}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (a_n^2 + b_n^2) \right] $$

14. Identity in (-π, π)

$$ \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} [f(x)]^2 \,dx = \pi \left[ \frac{a_0^2}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (a_n^2 + b_n^2) \right] $$

15. Identity in (0, 2L)

$$ \int_{0}^{2L} [f(x)]^2 \,dx = L \left[ \frac{a_0^2}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (a_n^2 + b_n^2) \right] $$

16. Identity in (-L, L)

$$ \int_{-L}^{L} [f(x)]^2 \,dx = L \left[ \frac{a_0^2}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (a_n^2 + b_n^2) \right] $$

17. Identity for Half-Range Cosine Series in (0, π)

$$ \int_{0}^{\pi} [f(x)]^2 \,dx = \frac{\pi}{2} \left[ \frac{a_0^2}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n^2 \right] $$

18. Identity for Half-Range Sine Series in (0, π)

$$ \int_{0}^{\pi} [f(x)]^2 \,dx = \frac{\pi}{2} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n^2 $$

19. Identity for Half-Range Cosine Series in (0, L)

$$ \int_{0}^{L} [f(x)]^2 \,dx = \frac{L}{2} \left[ \frac{a_0^2}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n^2 \right] $$

20. Identity for Half-Range Sine Series in (0, L)

$$ \int_{0}^{L} [f(x)]^2 \,dx = \frac{L}{2} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n^2 $$
