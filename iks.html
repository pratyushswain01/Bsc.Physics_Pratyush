Key Concepts for IKS Exam

Module 1: Introduction to IKS

Classification of Knowledge

  • Caturdasa Vidyasthanam: The 14 branches of knowledge (4 Vedas, 6 Vedangas, Puranas, Nyaya, Mimamsa, Dharmashastras).
  • 64 Kalas (Arts): The 64 traditional arts, showing a holistic approach to skills beyond academics.

Core Texts & Literature

  • The Four Vedas: Foundational scriptures (Rigveda, Yajurveda, Samaveda, Atharvaveda).
  • Vedangas: Six auxiliary disciplines to understand Vedas. Note the Sulbasutras for early geometry.
  • Itihasa: The two great epics - Ramayana and Mahabharata, as sources of history and dharma.
  • Puranas: Texts with myths, genealogies, and cosmology.

Schools of Philosophy (Darshanas) - VERY IMPORTANT

  • Vedic/Orthodox (Astika): Accept Vedas. Know the six schools:
    1. Sankhya: Duality of consciousness (Purusha) and matter (Prakriti).
    2. Yoga: Practical path to liberation based on controlling the mind.
    3. Nyaya: School of logic and reasoning.
    4. Vaisesika: School of atomism.
    5. Purva-Mimamsa: Focus on Vedic rituals.
    6. Vedanta: Philosophy of the Upanishads.
  • Non-Vedic/Heterodox (Nastika): Reject Vedas.
    • Carvaka: Materialism.
    • Buddhist Philosophy: Four Noble Truths, Eightfold Path.
    • Jain Philosophy: Non-violence (Ahimsa), non-absolutism (Anekantavada).

Module 2: Foundation for Science & Technology

Sanskrit & Linguistics

  • Panini's Astadhyayi: A highly systematic Sanskrit grammar, foundational for modern Natural Language Processing (NLP).

Number System & Mathematics

  • Concept of Zero (Shunya): India's key contribution. A dual role as a placeholder and a number.
  • Decimal System: The base-10 place-value system we use today.
  • Alternative Number Representations: Katapayadi System (mapping letters to numbers in verses) and Bhuta Samkhya System (using words with number associations).

Logic and Epistemology

  • Pramana: Means of valid knowledge. Focus on: Pratyaksha (Perception), Anumana (Inference), and Shabda (Testimony/Word of experts).
  • Pingala and the Binary System: Acharya Pingala's work on meters (Chandahshastra) used long/short syllables in a way that is equivalent to the binary system.

Module 3: Indian Mathematics & Astronomy

Great Mathematicians & Contributions

  • Aryabhata: Gave accurate value of Pi (π), developed trigonometry (sine tables), and proposed Earth's rotation.
  • Brahmagupta: Rules for operating with zero and negative numbers.
  • Bhaskara II: Precursor concepts to differential calculus.
  • Madhava: Founder of Kerala School; developed infinite series for sine/cosine, laying groundwork for calculus.

Key Concepts

  • Sulbasutras: Earliest source of geometry, contains the Pythagorean theorem.
  • Trigonometry: Development of Jya (Sine) and Kojya (Cosine) functions.
  • Pancanga: The 5-part traditional Indian calendar.
  • Jantar Mantar: Massive astronomical observatories (Yantras) for naked-eye observation.

Module 4: Indian Science & Technology Heritage

Metallurgy and Materials Science

  • Iron & Steel: The rust-proof Iron Pillar of Delhi and legendary Wootz Steel (used for Damascus blades).
  • Zinc Distillation: India was the first to master the industrial-scale distillation of Zinc.
  • Dyes & Painting: Long-lasting murals at the Ajanta Caves show advanced chemistry.

Architecture & Town Planning

  • Town Planning: Sophisticated grid layouts and drainage systems in the Harappan Civilization.
  • Vastu Sastra: Traditional science of architecture for aligning buildings with nature.
  • Temple Architecture: Know the two styles: Nagara (North, with Shikhara) and Dravida (South, with Vimana).

Module 5: Humanities & Social Sciences

Health and Wellness

  • Ayurveda: Traditional medicine based on balancing the three Doshas: Vata, Pitta, and Kapha.
  • Yoga: A holistic system for uniting the body, mind, and consciousness.

Psychology

  • The Triguna System: A model for personality and mental states based on three qualities:
    1. Sattva: Purity, balance, harmony.
    2. Rajas: Action, passion, restlessness.
    3. Tamas: Inertia, ignorance, laziness.

Governance and Management

  • Kautilya's Arthashastra: The definitive text on statecraft, economic policy, and military strategy.
  • Concept of "Rama Rajya": An ideal of a just state where the ruler is dedicated to the people's welfare.

Final Tip: Try to understand the connections! For example, how Panini's grammar (Module 2) enabled scientific precision, and how the Triguna system (Module 5) is applied in Ayurveda (Module 5). Good luck!
