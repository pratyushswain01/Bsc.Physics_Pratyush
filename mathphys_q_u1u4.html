UNIT 1: Fourier Series

Part B

  1. If \( f(x) = \begin{cases} \cos(x), & 0 < x < \pi \\ 50, & \pi < x < 2\pi \end{cases} \) and \( f(x) = f(x+2\pi) \) for all \(x\), find the sum of the Fourier series of \(f(x)\) at \(x = \pi\).
  2. The Fourier series expansion of \( f(x) \) in \( (0, 2\pi) \) is \( f(x) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{\sin(nx)}{n} \). Find the root mean square value of \(f(x)\) in the interval \( (0, 2\pi) \).
  3. Let \(f(x)\) be defined in \( (0, 2\pi) \) by \( f(x) = \begin{cases} 1 + \cos(x), & 0 < x < \pi \\ \cos(x), & \pi < x < 2\pi \end{cases} \) and \(f(x+2\pi)=f(x)\). Find the value of the series at \(x=\pi\).
  4. If \( \cos^3(t) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (a_n \cos(nt) + b_n \sin(nt)) \) in \( 0 \le t \le 2\pi \). Find the sum of the series \( \frac{a_0^2}{4} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (a_n^2 + b_n^2) \).
  5. Find the root mean square value of the function \(f(x) = x\) in the interval \((0, l)\) & for \(f(x) = x^2\).
  6. Without finding the values of \(a_0, a_n, b_n\), find the value of \( \frac{a_0^2}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (a_n^2 + b_n^2) \) for the function \( f(x) = x^2 \) in the interval \( (0, \pi) \).
  7. Find the root mean square value of the function \( f(x) = x^2 \) in the interval \( (0, \pi) \).
  8. If \( x^2 = \frac{\pi^2}{3} + 4 \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n}{n^2} \cos(nx) \), deduce that \( \frac{1}{1^2} + \frac{1}{2^2} + \frac{1}{3^2} + \dots = \frac{\pi^2}{6} \).
  9. Find the Half Range cosine series of \( f(x) = \cos(x) \), for \( 0 < x < \pi \).
  10. Write the coefficients \( a_0, a_n \) in the expansion of \( x + x^3 \) as a Fourier series in \( (-\pi, \pi) \).
  11. Determine the Fourier series for the function \( f(x) = x^2 \) of period \( 2\pi \) in \( 0 < x < 2\pi \). Hence deduce \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6} \).
  12. Expand in Fourier series \( f(x) = x \sin(x) \) for \( 0 < x < 2\pi \). Hence deduce the result \( \frac{1}{1 \cdot 3} - \frac{1}{3 \cdot 5} + \frac{1}{5 \cdot 7} - \dots = \frac{\pi - 2}{4} \).
  13. Find the Fourier series expansion of the periodic function \( f(x) \) of period \( 2\pi \) defined by \( f(x) = \begin{cases} \pi + x, & -\pi \le x \le 0 \\ \pi - x, & 0 \le x \le \pi \end{cases} \). Hence deduce that \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{(2n-1)^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{8} \).
  14. Find the Fourier series expansion of \( f(x) = \begin{cases} x, & 0 \le x \le \pi \\ 2\pi - x, & \pi \le x \le 2\pi \end{cases} \). Also deduce that \( \frac{1}{1^2} + \frac{1}{3^2} + \frac{1}{5^2} + \dots = \frac{\pi^2}{8} \).

UNIT 2: Partial Differential Equations

Part B

  1. Form the partial differential equation by eliminating constants \(a\) and \(b\) from \( (x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2 = z^2 \cot^2(\alpha) \).
  2. Form the partial differential equation of all spheres whose centre lies on the z-axis.
  3. Form the partial differential equation of all planes having equal intercepts on the x and y axis.
  4. Form the partial differential equation by eliminating the arbitrary function from \( f(z^2 - xy, \frac{x}{z}) = 0 \).
  5. Form the partial differential equation by eliminating the function from the relation \( z = f(x^2 + y^2) + x + y \).
  6. Find the solution of \( px^2 + qy^2 = z^2 \).
  7. Find the complete integral of the PDE \( (1-x)p + (2-y)q = 3 - z \).
  8. Find the complete integral of \( \frac{z}{pq} = \frac{x}{q} + \frac{y}{p} \). (Note: OCR was ambiguous, this is a plausible interpretation).
  9. Find the complete solution of the PDE \( p^2 + q^2 - 4pq = 0 \).
  10. Find the complete integral of \( p - q = 0 \).
  11. Solve the PDE \( pq = x \).
  12. Find the complete integral of \( \sqrt{p} + \sqrt{q} = 1 \).
  13. Find the complete integral of \( p - y^2 = q + x^2 \).
  14. Form the partial differential equation by eliminating a and b from \( (x-a)^2 + (y-b)^2 + z^2 = c^2 \).
  15. Form the partial differential equation by eliminating arbitrary functions from \( z = xf(2x+y) + g(2x+y) \).
  16. Form the partial differential equation by eliminating arbitrary functions \(f\) and \(\phi\) from \( z = f(x+ct) + \phi(x-ct) \).
  17. Form the partial differential equation by eliminating arbitrary function g from \( g(x^2+y^2+z^2, xyz) = 0 \).
  18. Solve \( p(1-q^2) = q(1-z) \).
  19. Solve \( p(1-q) = qz \).
  20. Solve \( pq+p+q=0 \).
  21. Solve \( 9(p^2z + q^2) = 4 \).
